Counterproposal to PaaS Agreement

Proposed amendments to the Proprietary Trading Management Software Services Agreement

From: Neom Triple A Information Technology LLC To: Axcera LLC Date: April 2026

Introduction

Thank you for presenting the Proprietary Trading Management Software Services Agreement and the Multi-asset Appendix A. We have reviewed the full contract in detail and are interested in proceeding with Axcera as our technology partner for the expansion into Futures.

NEOMAAA is an established proprietary trading firm with a proven track record:

  • 40,000+ registered users
  • 3,400+ accounts created per month
  • $250,000+ monthly revenue
  • 16-person operational team
  • Currently operating on 3 CFD platforms (MT5 + TradeLocker)

We are ready to commit to a long-term partnership. However, we have identified several terms that require adjustment to reflect the scale of our business and the mutual value of this relationship. Below are our proposed amendments.

Our commitment: We are offering Axcera a client that will ramp from $15,000/month at launch to $30,000/month by month 6 as we add a second Futures platform and cross the $500K revenue threshold. Steady-state annual value: $367,500. Total 2-year value: $690,000+. The tiered structure ensures Axcera earns more as NEOMAAA grows — true alignment.

Proposed Amendments

1. Tiered Pricing Structure with Monthly Reset Required
Reference: Appendix A — Replaces MTSF + Revenue Trigger
Current: Fixed MTSF of $15,000/month for multi-asset package (up to 3 CFD), plus $5,000 per Futures platform, plus a permanent $10,000/month surcharge once revenue exceeds $250,000. The surcharge applies to all subsequent months regardless of whether revenue falls below the threshold.
Proposed: Replace the current fee structure with a single-tier, revenue-based model with monthly reset:

Base package (2 CFD + 1 Futures platform):
  • Revenue under $500,000 → $15,000/month
  • Revenue $500,000+ → $25,000/month
4th platform add-on: +$5,000/month added to the applicable tier above.

The surcharge resets monthly — it only applies in months where the revenue threshold is actually exceeded. If revenue drops below $500K in any given month, the Licensee reverts to the base fee.
Rationale: This structure is simpler, fairer, and aligns Axcera's revenue directly with NEOMAAA's success:
  • Axcera earns more when NEOMAAA earns more — true partnership alignment
  • 1 Futures platform included in the base — simplifies billing and removes friction for Futures adoption
  • Monthly reset eliminates the permanent ratchet — NEOMAAA pays the surcharge in growth months and gets relief in slower months. In practice, as we scale, the surcharge will be active most months
  • Higher threshold ($500K) — gives NEOMAAA 2–3 months of breathing room during migration. We currently generate $250K/month in CFD alone; combined with Futures, we project hitting $500K by month 3
NEOMAAA is not asking to avoid paying more — we are asking for a structure where we pay more when we are actually generating more.
2. MTSF Commencement Required
Reference: Section 3.3
Current: The MTSF shall commence on the earlier of (i) the Licensee going live (first paying customer), or (ii) thirty (30) calendar days after Axcera has provided access to the Platform.
Proposed: The MTSF shall commence only upon the Licensee going live, defined as having their first paying customer. Remove the 30-day automatic trigger.
Rationale: The migration and integration process requires testing, data migration, and team training. Charging the full MTSF before the platform is operational creates financial pressure to rush the launch, which is not in either party's interest. Tying commencement to the first paying customer ensures NEOMAAA launches properly, which benefits Axcera's long-term revenue.
3. Installation Fee Reduction Important
Reference: Appendix A — Installation Fee
Current: Installation Fee of $25,000 payable upon execution of the Agreement.
Proposed: Reduce Installation Fee to $15,000 – $20,000, reflecting the lower onboarding cost for an established client migrating from an existing platform.
Rationale: NEOMAAA is not a startup building from scratch. We have established operations, existing user base, and operational processes. The integration effort is a migration, not a greenfield setup. Additionally, Axcera will recover the setup cost within the first month of MTSF payments. A reduced installation fee of $15,000–$20,000 reflects the actual onboarding work required.
4. Non-Compete Clause Required
Reference: Section 6.3
Current: 2 years post-termination. Covers any platform, software or service that "competes" with anything Axcera offers — extremely broad scope.
Proposed: Reduce the post-termination non-compete period to six (6) months. Restrict scope to direct replication of Axcera's specific proprietary technology — not a blanket prohibition on operating in the prop trading space. The non-compete shall not apply if Axcera terminates the agreement for non-cause or fails to meet SLA obligations.
Rationale: A 2-year blanket non-compete on an entire industry vertical is commercially disproportionate and difficult to enforce in most jurisdictions. Six months, limited to direct replication of Axcera's proprietary technology, protects Axcera's legitimate IP interests without restricting NEOMAAA's normal business operations.
5. Termination Notice Period Important
Reference: Section 9.2
Current: Axcera reserves the exclusive right to terminate the Agreement and revoke credentials with immediate effect, without prior notice, if Licensee engages in illegal or unethical activities.
Proposed: All termination scenarios require a minimum of 30 calendar days written notice and a right to cure within that period. Immediate termination only for criminal convictions, not subjective determinations.
Rationale: Immediate termination without notice or cure period puts NEOMAAA's entire operation at risk. A 30-day notice ensures business continuity and gives both parties the opportunity to resolve disputes. The term "unethical" is subjective; limiting immediate termination to criminal convictions provides objective criteria.
6. Service Level Agreement (SLA) New Clause
Reference: Article VII (currently "AS IS")
Current: All software is provided "AS IS" without warranty of any kind. Axcera does not warrant that the software is bug-free or that use will be uninterrupted.
Proposed: Add an SLA guaranteeing:
  • 99.5% monthly uptime for the Platform during market hours
  • 4-hour response time for critical incidents affecting trading operations
  • MTSF credits for any month where uptime falls below 99.5%
Rationale: At $20,000+/month, a service with zero performance guarantees creates unacceptable operational risk. An SLA is standard in enterprise software agreements at this price point and demonstrates Axcera's confidence in their platform.
7. Discretionary Advance Payment Important
Reference: Section 3.2(c)
Current: Axcera can unilaterally demand up to three (3) months of fees upfront based on its own internal assessment of "financial risk." No objective criteria defined. Five (5) business days to pay or face immediate suspension.
Proposed: Remove this clause entirely. Alternatively, replace with a strictly defined trigger — advance payment only applicable after two consecutive missed payments, with 10 business days to cure.
Rationale: As written, this is a subjective pressure tool with no checks. No SaaS agreement grants a vendor this level of unilateral discretion without objective thresholds. NEOMAAA is an established, revenue-generating business; tying the clause to documented payment delinquency provides a fair, objective trigger.
8. Resumption Fee After Suspension Important
Reference: Section 3.2(d)
Current: Upon resuming service after non-payment suspension, Axcera adds its "reasonable expenses" for stoppage and re-engagement on top of the outstanding balance. No cap, no definition of what qualifies.
Proposed: Remove entirely. Resumption of service shall occur automatically upon settlement of the outstanding balance, with no additional charges.
Rationale: The Licensee is already paying MTSF for the platform. Charging a reinstatement fee on top of cleared arrears is a double penalty with no market precedent in standard SaaS agreements.
9. Late Payment Interest Important
Reference: Section 5.3
Current: 18% per annum on overdue balances after 10 business days.
Proposed: Reduce to 8% per annum.
Rationale: 18% is punitive and above standard commercial rates. 8% adequately compensates Axcera for late payment without functioning as a penalty clause.
10. Early Termination Right by Licensee Required
Reference: Section 9.1
Current: No early termination right exists for the Licensee. The only exit options are material breach by Axcera or Axcera's bankruptcy. Outside of these, the Licensee is locked in for the full annual term with no exit.
Proposed: Add an Early Termination Clause — Licensee may terminate at any time with 30 days written notice, paying the current month plus one additional month as a termination fee. No further obligations after that period.
Rationale: A 12-month lock-in with no exit right is unusually restrictive for a SaaS licensing agreement. The proposed 30-day notice plus one month's fee is a reasonable commercial compromise that protects Axcera's revenue while giving the Licensee a proportionate exit mechanism.
11. Auto-Renewal Notice Period Important
Reference: Section 9.1
Current: 60 days written notice required to prevent automatic renewal.
Proposed: Reduce to 30 days.
Rationale: 60 days is excessive for an annual contract. 30 days is the market standard.
12. Data Return on Termination New Clause
Reference: New clause — Post-Termination Obligations
Current: The Agreement contains no explicit mechanism requiring Axcera to return or export Licensee client data, account configurations or platform settings upon termination.
Proposed: Add an explicit clause: upon termination, Axcera shall provide a full export of all Licensee client data, account configurations and platform settings within 14 calendar days at no additional charge, in a commonly readable format.
Rationale: Client data belongs to the Licensee by definition. Without an explicit data return clause, there is no contractual mechanism to recover operational data after exit. This clause protects NEOMAAA's continuity of operations and is standard in enterprise SaaS/PaaS agreements.
13. Technical Commitments — Integration Scope New Clause
Reference: New Annex — derived from Axcera Q&A dated 19 November 2025
Current: The Agreement does not codify the integration capabilities committed verbally by Axcera during pre-contract due diligence. These commitments must be binding to protect NEOMAAA's integration roadmap.
Proposed: Add a technical annex formalizing the following commitments confirmed by Axcera:
  • WooCommerce compatibility — current checkout and affiliate system (WooCommerce-based) shall remain operative and integrated with the Platform.
  • Email provider — Brevo integration supported out of the box; no additional fee for connection.
  • APIs — REST APIs provided in pull-based read-only mode, covering at minimum:
    • List of all account plans with details
    • CRUD of users
    • CRUD of trading accounts (with account plan and user reference)
    • Account positions
    • Account trades
    • CRUD of user/account payouts
    • CRUD of competitions
  • Webhooks — full list of available webhooks to be delivered upon agreement execution and documented as a contractual annex.
  • Database access — access via CRM or API available to Licensee for back-office operations, custom dashboards and reporting.
  • Payment providers — any PSP with a WooCommerce plugin supported at no extra cost. Custom PSP integrations billable separately with written quote and Licensee's prior approval.
  • Data hosting — databases hosted on AWS, with data management terms per the draft agreement.
  • Data export — Licensee may export account histories, deals and stage transitions for risk management purposes without additional charge.
Rationale: Axcera confirmed each of the above in writing via the "NeomAAA Tech Questions" document (last updated 19 November 2025). Codifying these answers as contractual commitments converts verbal assurances into enforceable obligations. Without this annex, NEOMAAA's integration roadmap depends on informal promises that carry no legal weight after signature.
14. Jurisdiction Accepted
Reference: Section 12.2
Accepted as is: Dubai courts as jurisdiction, given that the Licensee entity (Neom Triple A Information Technology LLC) is based in Dubai.
Rationale: No change requested. Jurisdiction aligns with the Licensee's domicile and presents no operational or legal friction.

Red Lines — Non-Negotiable

The following four items are non-negotiable conditions for NEOMAAA to execute this Agreement. Each represents a structural protection of the Licensee's operational continuity and cannot be waived.

  • Early Termination Clause is non-negotiable — 30 days notice, one month fee, clean exit.
  • Non-compete must not exceed 6 months post-termination.
  • Advance payment clause must be removed or objectively defined (tied to documented payment delinquency).
  • Data return upon termination must be explicitly guaranteed (14 calendar days, no additional charge).
